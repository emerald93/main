Net Sales

The amount of sales generated by a store after the deduction of returns, purchased gift cards, licenses, store use and donations.

The sales number is reported on the store's financial statements as SALES - CASH/CHECK/OTHER, reflecting these deductions.

Gross Margin

A store's net sales minus its cost of goods sold. The gross margin represents the total sales revenue that the company retains after incurring the direct costs associated with producing the goods and services sold by a company. The higher the percentage, the more the company retains on each dollar of sales to service its other costs and obligation.

If you subtract gross margin from net sales, you will get the POS - COGS (Point of Sale - Cost of Goods Sold) line on the store's financial statement

Units

The number of units sold by a store that makes up the net sales figure.

This Year

Corresponds to the dates selected in the date section. Examples listed below assume today is July 18th, 2015
  • Specific Date - a single individual date on the calendar
    Example: 07/12/2015
  • Custom Date Range - a specific beginning date and ending date, inclusive of all the dates between
    Example: 07/04/2015 - 07/09/2015
  • Week to Date - The prior Sunday of the current week you are in through yesterday
    Example: 07/12/2015 - 07/17/2015
  • Month to Date - The first of the month through yesterday
    Example: 07/01/2015 - 07/17/2015
  • Previous Month - The first of the month for the month selected through the last day of that same month
    Example: 05/01/2015 - 05/31/2015
  • Year to Date - The first of January through yesterday
    Example: 01/01/2015 - 07/17/2015
  • Previous Year - the first day of the year for the year selected through the last day of that same year
    Example: 01/01/2014 - 12/31/2014

Last Year

Corresponds to the relative dates selected in the date section for the prior year. Examples listed below are for the This Year dates selected above
  • Specific Date - the same day of the year for the year prior to the selected date
    Example: if this year = 7/12/2015, then last year = 07/13/2014 (both are a Sunday)
  • Custom Date Range - the same day of the year for the year prior to the selected beginning date and ending date, inclusive of all the dates between
    Example: if this year = 07/04/2015 - 07/09/2015, then last year = 07/05/2014 - 07/10/2014 (both are Saturday - Thursday)
  • Week to Date - the same day of the year for the year prior to the selected Sunday, ending the same day of the year that corresponds with yesterdays day
    Example: if this year = 07/12/2015 - 07/17/2015, then last year = 07/13/2014 - 07/18/2014 (both are a Sunday- Friday)
  • Month to Date - the first of the month for the year prior through the same date as yesterday for the current year
    Example:if this year = 07/01/2015 - 07/17/2015, then last year = 07/01/2014 - 07/17/2014 (both are the 1st - 17th)
  • Previous Month - The first of the month for the month selected through the last day of that same month for the prior year
    Example: if this year = 05/01/2015 - 05/31/2015, then last year = 05/01/2014 - 05/31/2014 (both are the 1st - 31st of the same month)
  • Year to Date - The first of January through yesterday for the prior year
    Example: if this year = 01/01/2015 - 07/17/2015, then last year = 01/01/2014 - 07/17/2014 (both are the 1st - 17th)
  • Previous Year - the first day of the prior year for the year selected through the last day of that same year
    Example: if this year = 01/01/2014 - 12/31/2014, then last year = 01/01/2013 - 12/31/2013 (both are for the entire year)

Dollar Change

The difference between the number that corresponds with this year and the number that corresponds with last year. If this is a positive number, that means you have performed better than the year prior. A negative number means you have decreased in performance over the year prior.

Percent Change

Percent Change = (((This Year / Last Year) - 1) * 100)
